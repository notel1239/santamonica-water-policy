# Santa Monica Sustainable Water Management Policy

## 1. Introduction
The City of Santa Monica is committed to sustainable water management to ensure long-term water security, environmental protection, and resilience to climate change. This policy outlines guidelines for residential, commercial, and municipal water use.

## 2. Residential Water Use
- **Water Conservation Targets:** Reduce per capita residential water consumption by 20% by 2030.
- **Rainwater Harvesting:** Encourage installation of rainwater collection systems through rebate programs.
- **Efficient Fixtures:** Mandate use of WaterSense labeled fixtures in new construction and major renovations.
- **Outdoor Watering:** Implement seasonal watering schedules and promote drought-tolerant landscaping.

## 3. Commercial Water Use
- **Water Audits:** Require annual water audits for commercial properties over 50,000 sq ft.
- **Cooling Towers:** Enforce water‑efficiency standards for cooling towers and evaporative cooling systems.
- **Water Fountains:** All new or renovated ornamental fountains must use recirculating systems and be equipped with water‑saving controls.
- **Reuse Systems:** Incentivize graywater and reclaimed water systems for non‑potable uses.

## 4. Municipal Water Use
- **Lead by Example:** Municipal facilities shall achieve a 25% reduction in water use by 2030.
- **Infrastructure Upgrades:** Prioritize replacement of aging pipelines to reduce leaks.
- **Public Parks:** Install smart irrigation controllers and use drought‑resistant plant species.
- **Education and Outreach:** Develop public campaigns to raise awareness about water conservation.

## 5. Monitoring and Compliance
- **Reporting:** Annual water use reporting for all sectors.
- **Enforcement:** Progressive penalties for non‑compliance with provisions of this policy.
- **Review:** This policy will be reviewed every five years to incorporate new technologies and climate data.
